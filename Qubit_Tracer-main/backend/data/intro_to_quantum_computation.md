# Global Perspectives

Quantum computation and quantum information is the study of the information processing tasks that can be accomplished using quantum mechanical systems.

Sounds pretty simple and obvious, doesn’t it? Like many simple but profound ideas, it was a long time before anybody thought of doing information processing using quantum mechanical systems.

---

## Why This Took Time to Develop

To see why this is the case, we must go back in time and look in turn at each of the fields which have contributed fundamental ideas to quantum computation and quantum information:

1. **Quantum Mechanics** – The foundation for understanding how particles behave at microscopic scales.
2. **Computer Science** – The study of algorithms, computation models, and complexity.
3. **Information Theory** – How data can be represented, stored, and transmitted efficiently.
4. **Cryptography** – Securing communication and information from unauthorized access.

---

## A Historical Tour

As we take our short historical tour of these fields, think of yourself in each role:

- **First as a Physicist** – Understanding the physical principles behind quantum systems.
- **Then as a Computer Scientist** – Exploring how quantum phenomena can improve computation.
- **Then as an Information Theorist** – Considering data encoding and transmission in a quantum framework.
- **Finally as a Cryptographer** – Leveraging quantum properties for secure communication.

These perspectives have come together to shape the modern fields of **quantum computation** and **quantum information**.
